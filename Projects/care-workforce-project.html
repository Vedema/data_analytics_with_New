Care Workforce Analytics Dashboard

An Excel-based workforce performance dashboard designed to support operational decision-making within a care organisation.

Case Study

A care organisation required an analytical solution to monitor workforce performance, identify risk patterns, and understand operational workload distribution.

Management wanted answers to key operational questions:

  • Which staff roles make up the largest proportion of the workforce?
  • How much overtime is being worked?
  • Are there patterns in absence and lateness?
  • Which staff may be at operational risk?

Dataset Overview

The dataset included workforce records such as:

  • Staff ID
  • Department
  • Shift Type
  • Absence Days
  • Late Days
  • Overtime Hours
  • Contract Type

The raw dataset contained inconsistencies including duplicate entries, formatting issues, and inconsistent text values.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • Microsoft Excel (Desktop)
  • Data Cleaning Functions (TRIM, PROPER, VALUE, DATEVALUE)
  • Logical Functions (IF, AND, OR)
  • COUNTIF, SUMIF, AVERAGEIF
  • Pivot Tables
  • Charts & Visualisations
  • Slicers for Interactive Filtering

Data Cleaning Process

  • Removed duplicate records.
  • Standardised text formatting (Shift types, Departments).
  • Converted text numbers into numeric values.
  • Created cleaned columns for analysis.
  • Generated calculated fields (RiskFlag, OvertimeLevel).

Analysis & Dashboard Development

After cleaning the dataset, KPI metrics were generated:

  • Total Staff Count
  • Staff At Risk
  • Total Overtime Hours
  • Average Absence Days

Pivot tables were created to analyse:

  • Role distribution
  • Overtime levels by department
  • Risk classification patterns

Visual charts and slicers were added to create an interactive dashboard.

Key Insights

  • Certain departments showed significantly higher overtime levels.
  • A small percentage of staff were flagged as operational risk due to absence and lateness patterns.
  • Shift distribution patterns indicated potential workload imbalance.
  • Data-driven insights can support improved workforce planning.
