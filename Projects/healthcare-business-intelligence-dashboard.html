Project Overview

This project demonstrates the complete design and implementation of an interactive Healthcare Business Intelligence dashboard using Microsoft Power BI. Starting with a raw healthcare dataset, Power Query was used to extract, clean and transform the data before building analytical measures with DAX and designing a fully interactive executive dashboard.

The dashboard enables healthcare stakeholders to monitor patient admissions, treatment costs, departmental performance, patient satisfaction and geographical trends through intuitive data visualisations. By combining ETL, data modelling and interactive reporting, the project transforms raw operational data into meaningful business intelligence that supports evidence-based decision-making.

Business Problem

Healthcare organisations generate thousands of operational records every day, including patient admissions, treatment costs, departmental activity, patient satisfaction and demographic information. However, raw operational datasets often contain missing values, inconsistent text formatting, duplicate records and invalid data that reduce the reliability of reporting and business decision-making.

The objective of this project was to transform the raw healthcare dataset into a reliable analytical model capable of supporting interactive reporting and evidence-based healthcare management using Microsoft Power BI.

Power Query ETL Process

Microsoft Power Query was used to perform the complete Extract, Transform and Load (ETL) process. This stage ensured that the healthcare dataset was accurate, consistent and ready for business intelligence reporting. Each transformation improved data quality before the dataset was loaded into the Power BI model.

Step 1 — Launch Power Query

The imported healthcare dataset was opened in Microsoft Power Query Editor, providing a dedicated environment for performing all ETL operations before loading the cleaned data into the Power BI model.

  • Review imported columns.
  • Inspect data types.
  • Identify quality issues.
  • Prepare transformation workflow.
Business Value: Power Query creates a repeatable data preparation process that can automatically refresh whenever new healthcare data becomes available.

Step 2 — Standardising Gender Values

Patient gender values were reviewed and standardised to ensure consistent categorisation across the dataset. Cleaning categorical variables improves filtering, grouping and visualisation accuracy throughout the Power BI dashboard.

  • Removed inconsistent text values.
  • Standardised Male and Female categories.
  • Improved slicer accuracy.
  • Enhanced demographic reporting.
Business Value: Consistent gender categories improve reporting reliability and ensure demographic analyses accurately reflect the underlying patient population.

Step 3 — Cleaning Department Names

Department names were standardised to eliminate inconsistent spellings and duplicate categories. Standardisation ensures departments appear correctly within Power BI visuals and prevents inaccurate aggregations.

  • Corrected inconsistent department names.
  • Improved grouping accuracy.
  • Prevented duplicate categories.
  • Enhanced departmental comparisons.
Business Value: Clean department labels enable accurate operational reporting and fair comparison of departmental performance across the organisation.

Step 4 — Cleaning Admission Type

Admission type categories were standardised to remove inconsistencies between emergency, routine and elective admissions. This ensures accurate patient segmentation and meaningful dashboard filtering.

  • Validated admission categories.
  • Removed inconsistent entries.
  • Prepared for slicers.
  • Improved reporting consistency.
Business Value: Consistent admission classifications allow hospital managers to compare emergency and planned admissions more effectively.

Step 5 — Handling Missing Satisfaction Scores

Missing values within the Satisfaction Score column were addressed using mean imputation. The average satisfaction score was calculated and then used to replace missing values, ensuring the dataset remained complete while preserving the overall distribution of patient satisfaction.

  • Calculated the mean satisfaction score.
  • Replaced missing values using the calculated mean.
  • Maintained dataset completeness.
  • Improved dashboard reliability.
Business Value: Completing missing satisfaction scores allows healthcare managers to accurately monitor patient experience without excluding incomplete records from the analysis.

Step 6 — Replacing Missing Treatment Costs

Treatment Cost contained missing values that could distort financial reporting. Since treatment costs may contain extreme values, the median was chosen instead of the mean because it is less sensitive to outliers.

  • Calculated the median treatment cost.
  • Replaced missing treatment costs.
  • Reduced the influence of outliers.
  • Improved financial reporting accuracy.
Business Value: Using the median provides a more representative replacement value for healthcare costs, resulting in more reliable financial KPIs and expenditure analysis.

DAX Measures & KPI Development

After completing the ETL process, custom Data Analysis Expressions (DAX) measures were created to calculate the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) displayed throughout the dashboard. DAX enables dynamic calculations that automatically respond to filters, slicers and user interactions, making it one of the most powerful features of Microsoft Power BI.

The measures developed in this project provide meaningful business metrics including total treatment costs, average treatment costs, patient volumes and patient satisfaction scores. These calculations form the foundation of the dashboard and allow healthcare managers to monitor organisational performance in real time.

KPI 1 — Total Treatment Cost

This DAX measure calculates the total treatment expenditure across all patients within the healthcare dataset. It provides an overall financial overview of hospital activity and forms one of the primary executive KPIs displayed on the dashboard.

  • Uses the SUM() aggregation function.
  • Calculates total expenditure.
  • Updates dynamically when filters are applied.
  • Supports departmental cost analysis.
Business Value: Provides healthcare managers with an immediate overview of total hospital treatment expenditure, supporting budgeting and financial monitoring.

KPI 2 — Average Treatment Cost

The average treatment cost measure calculates the mean cost per patient. This metric helps identify variations in treatment expenditure and enables comparisons between departments or patient groups.

  • Uses the AVERAGE() function.
  • Calculates average patient cost.
  • Supports comparative financial analysis.
  • Responds to slicers and filters.
Business Value: Average treatment cost enables hospitals to benchmark spending across departments and identify unusually high treatment expenses.

KPI 3 — Total Patients

This measure counts the total number of patient records contained within the cleaned dataset. Patient volume provides an important operational indicator that supports capacity planning and departmental workload analysis.

  • Counts all patient records.
  • Provides operational workload information.
  • Supports admission analysis.
  • Updates dynamically with dashboard filters.
Business Value: Patient count helps healthcare organisations understand service demand and resource utilisation across departments.

KPI 4 — Average Satisfaction Score

The satisfaction score measure calculates the average patient satisfaction rating. This KPI enables healthcare organisations to monitor service quality and evaluate patient experience across departments and demographic groups.

  • Calculates average satisfaction rating.
  • Measures service quality.
  • Supports patient experience analysis.
  • Responds dynamically to slicers.
Business Value: Monitoring patient satisfaction enables hospitals to identify areas requiring service improvement while maintaining high standards of patient care.

Why DAX Measures Matter

Unlike static calculations in spreadsheets, DAX measures are evaluated dynamically whenever users interact with the dashboard. Selecting different departments, admission types, cities or patient demographics automatically recalculates every KPI, ensuring that decision-makers always view accurate and context-specific information. This flexibility transforms raw healthcare data into an interactive Business Intelligence solution capable of supporting operational, financial and strategic decision-making.

Interactive Healthcare Dashboard

After completing the ETL process and creating the required DAX measures, the cleaned dataset was transformed into an interactive Business Intelligence dashboard using Microsoft Power BI. The dashboard provides healthcare managers with a single view of organisational performance through dynamic KPI cards, charts, maps and slicers.

Users can filter the dashboard by department, city, admission type, gender and patient outcome, allowing decision-makers to investigate specific operational areas without modifying the underlying data model.

Executive KPI Summary

Total Treatment Cost

The dashboard reports a total treatment expenditure of approximately £4.9M, representing the cumulative cost of treating all patients included within the dataset.

This KPI provides executives with an immediate financial overview and acts as a benchmark when comparing expenditure across departments or monitoring future reporting periods.

Business Insight: A relatively high total expenditure suggests significant resource utilisation across hospital departments. Decision-makers should investigate which services contribute most to overall spending.

Average Treatment Cost

The average treatment cost is approximately £5.03k per patient. This measure provides insight into the typical cost associated with delivering healthcare services.

Comparing this value between departments allows managers to identify unusually expensive treatment pathways and opportunities for improving operational efficiency.

Business Insight: Departments with treatment costs significantly above the organisational average should be reviewed to determine whether higher expenditure reflects greater clinical complexity or operational inefficiencies.

Patient Volume

The dashboard reports a total of 974 patients, representing the number of patient records included in the healthcare dataset after the ETL process. This KPI provides an immediate indication of hospital activity and forms the basis for analysing departmental workload, treatment costs and patient outcomes.

Business Insight: Monitoring patient volume alongside financial and operational KPIs enables healthcare managers to understand demand for services and evaluate whether available resources are sufficient to meet patient needs.

Average Satisfaction Score

The average patient satisfaction score is approximately 5.35 out of 10, indicating moderate levels of patient satisfaction across the healthcare organisation.

Although satisfaction remains relatively stable, the score highlights opportunities for improving patient experience and service quality.

Business Insight: Improving patient communication, reducing waiting times and enhancing post-treatment support may contribute to higher satisfaction scores in future reporting periods.

Department Performance Analysis

Operational Analysis

The visual indicates that treatment costs vary considerably across all age groups. While older patients frequently incur higher treatment costs, there is no perfect linear relationship, suggesting that clinical complexity and treatment type also contribute significantly to expenditure.
